    # Abstract

    The presence of reflectors in the environment surrounding a transmitter and receiver create multiple paths that a transmitted signal can traverse.

    As a result, the receiver sees the superposition (opens new window) of multiple copies of the transmitted signal, each traversing a different path. Each signal copy will experience differences in attenuation (opens new window), delay (opens new window) and phase shift (opens new window) while travelling from the source to the receiver. This can result in either constructive or destructive interference, amplifying or attenuating the signal power seen at the receiver. Strong destructive interference is frequently referred to as a deep fade and may result in temporary failure of communication due to a severe drop in the channel signal-to-noise ratio (opens new window).

    # AoA

    Angle of arrival(AoA)[^1], AoA determines the direction by measuring the Time Difference of Arrival (TDOA (opens new window)) at individual elements of the array -- from these delays the AoA can be calculated.

    Generally this TDOA measurement is made by measuring the difference in received phase at each element in the antenna array.
